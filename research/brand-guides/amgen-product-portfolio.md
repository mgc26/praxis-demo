## Product 1: Repatha (evolocumab)

| Field | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Brand / Generic** | Repatha / evolocumab |
| **Therapeutic area** | Cardiovascular |
| **FY2025 revenue** | $3,016M (+36% YoY) |
| **Mechanism** | Human monoclonal antibody; PCSK9 inhibitor. Binds PCSK9, prevents LDL receptor degradation, increases LDL-C clearance from blood. |
| **Indications** | (1) Reduce risk of major adverse CV events (CV death, MI, stroke, hospitalization for unstable angina, coronary revascularization) in adults at increased risk. (2) Adjunct to diet/exercise to reduce LDL-C in adults with primary hyperlipidemia. (3) Adults and pediatric patients >=10 years with HeFH. (4) Adults and pediatric patients >=10 years with HoFH. As of Aug 2025: now indicated for adults at increased risk for MACE due to uncontrolled LDL-C. |
| **Patient population** | Adults with ASCVD or high CV risk on maximally tolerated statin therapy who need additional LDL-C lowering. Also familial hypercholesterolemia patients (HeFH/HoFH) age 10+. |
| **Route** | Subcutaneous injection. 140 mg every 2 weeks OR 420 mg once monthly. SureClick autoinjector or prefilled syringe. |
| **Prescribing HCP specialties** | Cardiologists, lipidologists, endocrinologists, primary care/internal medicine |
| **Key screening/monitoring** | Fasting lipid panel (LDL-C baseline and periodic monitoring). LDL-C goal attainment assessment. CV risk stratification (10-year ASCVD risk score). Liver function tests if hepatotoxicity suspected. HeFH/HoFH genetic testing for familial forms. |
| **Common adverse events** | Nasopharyngitis, upper respiratory tract infection, injection site reactions (erythema, pain, bruising), back pain, influenza-like symptoms, urinary tract infections, muscle pain/spasm, diarrhea, dizziness. Serious: allergic reactions (rare). |
| **Patient support programs** | Amgen SupportPlus (1-844-REPATHA / 1-844-737-2842). Repatha Co-Pay Card ($0 copay for eligible commercially insured). AmgenNow direct-to-patient program ($239/month). Amgen Safety Net Foundation (free drug for qualifying uninsured). Amgen Nurse Partners. |
| **Competitors** | **Praluent (alirocumab)** -- Regeneron/Sanofi. Same class (PCSK9 mAb), SC every 2-4 weeks. **Leqvio (inclisiran)** -- Novartis. siRNA targeting PCSK9 mRNA. Twice-yearly injection (after loading). ~50% LDL-C reduction vs ~60% for Repatha. Major dosing convenience advantage. |

## Product 2: Prolia / XGEVA (denosumab)

### Prolia

| Field | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Brand / Generic** | Prolia / denosumab |
| **Therapeutic area** | Bone health / Osteoporosis |
| **FY2025 revenue** | ~$4,400M (+1% YoY) -- facing biosimilar erosion in 2026 |
| **Mechanism** | Human monoclonal antibody; RANK ligand (RANKL) inhibitor. Blocks RANKL, reduces osteoclast formation and activity, decreases bone resorption. |
| **Indications** | (1) Postmenopausal women with osteoporosis at high risk for fracture (history of osteoporotic fracture, multiple risk factors, or failed/intolerant to other therapy). (2) Increase bone mass in men with osteoporosis at high risk for fracture. (3) Treatment of glucocorticoid-induced osteoporosis. (4) Increase bone mass in men at high risk for fracture receiving androgen deprivation therapy for prostate cancer. (5) Increase bone mass in women at high risk for fracture receiving adjuvant aromatase inhibitor therapy for breast cancer. |
| **Patient population** | Postmenopausal women typically 55+. Men with osteoporosis. Cancer patients on hormone-depleting therapy. Patients on chronic glucocorticoids. |
| **Route** | Subcutaneous injection, 60 mg every 6 months. Administered in HCP office. |
| **Prescribing HCP specialties** | Rheumatologists, endocrinologists, orthopedic surgeons, primary care physicians, OB/GYNs, oncologists (for cancer-treatment-related bone loss) |
| **Key screening/monitoring** | DEXA scan (bone mineral density at baseline and periodically). Serum calcium, phosphorus, magnesium (before each dose and 10-14 days post-injection in patients predisposed to hypocalcemia). Renal function (CKD mineral bone disorder assessment). Vitamin D levels. Dental exam before initiation (ONJ risk). |
| **Common adverse events** | Back pain, pain in extremity, musculoskeletal pain, hypercholesterolemia, cystitis. **BOXED WARNING (2024): Severe hypocalcemia** -- fatal cases reported, especially in advanced CKD. Osteonecrosis of the jaw (ONJ). Atypical femoral fractures. Rebound vertebral fractures upon discontinuation. |
| **Patient support programs** | Amgen SupportPlus Co-Pay Program ($0 copay eligible). Bone Matters program (injection reminders, education). Amgen Nurse Partners. Amgen Safety Net Foundation. |
| **Competitors** | **Biosimilars (launched/launching 2025-2026):** Ospomyv (Samsung Bioepis), Conexxence (Fresenius Kabi), Steoboclo (Celltrion), Osvyrti (Accord/Intas), Boncresa (Amneal), Bosaya (Biocon), Bildyos (Organon) -- 8 biosimilar pairs approved in 2025. **Branded:** Reclast (zoledronic acid) IV bisphosphonate. Fosamax/Boniva (oral bisphosphonates). |

### XGEVA

| Field | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Brand / Generic** | XGEVA / denosumab |
| **Therapeutic area** | Oncology -- Bone metastases |
| **FY2025 revenue** | ~$1,800M (-6% YoY) |
| **Indications** | (1) Prevention of skeletal-related events (SREs) in patients with bone metastases from solid tumors. (2) Prevention of SREs in patients with multiple myeloma. (3) Treatment of giant cell tumor of bone (GCTB) in adults and skeletally mature adolescents. (4) Treatment of hypercalcemia of malignancy refractory to bisphosphonate therapy. |
| **Patient population** | Cancer patients with bone metastases (breast, prostate, lung, renal, others). Multiple myeloma patients. GCTB patients unresectable or where surgery would cause severe morbidity. |
| **Route** | Subcutaneous injection, 120 mg every 4 weeks. |
| **Prescribing HCP specialties** | Medical oncologists, radiation oncologists, urologists (prostate), orthopedic oncologists |
| **Key screening/monitoring** | Serum calcium (before each dose and during therapy, especially first weeks). Magnesium, phosphorus levels. Dental exam before initiation (ONJ risk). Renal function. |
| **Common adverse events** | Diarrhea, nausea, anemia, back pain, thrombocytopenia, peripheral edema, hypocalcemia, upper respiratory tract infection, rash, headache. Serious: osteonecrosis of the jaw (most common reason for discontinuation), pneumonia (most common serious AE). |
| **Patient support programs** | Amgen Assist 360 (Nurse Navigators, Reimbursement Counselors). Amgen FIRST STEP ($0 first dose, max $1,500/year copay assistance). Amgen Nurse Ambassadors. Amgen Safety Net Foundation. |
| **Competitors** | **Zometa (zoledronic acid)** -- Novartis. IV bisphosphonate, every 3-4 weeks. Generic available. **Biosimilars** -- same 8 XGEVA biosimilar pairs approved in 2025 (Xbryk, Bomyntra, Osenvelt, Jubereq, Oziltus, Aukelso, Bilprevda, etc.). |

## Product 3: TEZSPIRE (tezepelumab-ekko)

| Field | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Brand / Generic** | TEZSPIRE / tezepelumab-ekko |
| **Therapeutic area** | Inflammation -- Severe asthma / Nasal polyps |
| **FY2025 revenue** | ~$1,478M (+52% YoY) -- co-marketed with AstraZeneca |
| **Mechanism** | Human monoclonal antibody (IgG2-lambda); blocks thymic stromal lymphopoietin (TSLP). Inhibits upstream inflammatory signaling regardless of eosinophil phenotype. |
| **Indications** | (1) Add-on maintenance treatment of severe asthma in adults and pediatric patients aged 12+ years. NOT for acute bronchospasm or status asthmaticus. (2) Add-on maintenance for inadequately controlled chronic rhinosinusitis with nasal polyps (CRSwNP) in adults and pediatric patients 12+ (FDA approved Oct 2025). |
| **Patient population** | Adults and adolescents 12+ with severe asthma uncontrolled on inhaled corticosteroids + LABA. Unique positioning: works across all asthma phenotypes (eosinophilic, allergic, and non-eosinophilic/non-allergic). Also CRSwNP patients uncontrolled on standard therapy. |
| **Route** | Subcutaneous injection, 210 mg every 4 weeks. Prefilled syringe or autoinjector. Can be administered in HCP office or self-administered at home. |
| **Prescribing HCP specialties** | Pulmonologists, allergists/immunologists, ENT (for CRSwNP) |
| **Key screening/monitoring** | Blood eosinophil count (not required for eligibility, but used for phenotyping). FeNO (fractional exhaled nitric oxide). IgE levels. Spirometry (FEV1 at baseline and follow-up). Asthma exacerbation frequency. Oral corticosteroid dose tracking (for steroid-sparing). |
| **Common adverse events** | Pharyngitis, arthralgia, back pain, injection site reactions. Hypersensitivity reactions (rash, allergic conjunctivitis). Post-marketing: anaphylaxis reported (can be delayed onset, days after injection). Do not discontinue systemic or inhaled corticosteroids abruptly. Helminth infections: treat pre-existing parasitic infections before starting. |
| **Patient support programs** | Amgen SupportPlus. TEZSPIRE copay card ($0 copay for eligible commercially insured patients). Amgen Safety Net Foundation. |
| **Competitors** | **Dupixent (dupilumab)** -- Sanofi/Regeneron. Anti-IL-4R-alpha. Dominant market share. Also indicated for atopic dermatitis, CRSwNP, EoE. **Nucala (mepolizumab)** -- GSK. Anti-IL-5. Eosinophilic asthma only. **Fasenra (benralizumab)** -- AstraZeneca. Anti-IL-5R. Eosinophilic asthma only. **Xolair (omalizumab)** -- Genentech/Novartis. Anti-IgE. Allergic asthma. TEZSPIRE's differentiator: no biomarker requirement (works regardless of phenotype). |

## Product 4: Otezla (apremilast)

| Field | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Brand / Generic** | Otezla / apremilast |
| **Therapeutic area** | Dermatology / Rheumatology / Inflammation |
| **FY2025 revenue** | ~$2,300M (+7% YoY) |
| **Mechanism** | Oral PDE4 (phosphodiesterase 4) inhibitor. Increases intracellular cAMP, modulates inflammatory mediators. |
| **Indications** | (1) Adult patients with plaque psoriasis who are candidates for phototherapy or systemic therapy. (2) Pediatric patients 6+ years (>=20 kg) with moderate to severe plaque psoriasis. (3) Active psoriatic arthritis in adults and pediatric patients 6+ (>=20 kg). (4) Oral ulcers associated with Behcet's Disease in adults. First and only oral therapy approved across all plaque psoriasis severities. |
| **Patient population** | Adults with mild-to-severe plaque psoriasis. Psoriatic arthritis patients. Behcet's Disease patients with oral ulcers. Pediatric patients 6+ years. Often positioned for patients who prefer oral therapy over injections/infusions, or as a step before biologics. |
| **Route** | Oral tablet, 30 mg twice daily (after 5-day titration schedule). |
| **Prescribing HCP specialties** | Dermatologists, rheumatologists, primary care physicians |
| **Key screening/monitoring** | Body weight (for dose adjustment in severe renal impairment). Renal function (dose reduction to 30 mg once daily if CrCl <30 mL/min). Depression/suicidal ideation screening (monitor for mood changes). Weight monitoring (unexplained weight loss). |
| **Common adverse events** | Diarrhea (most common, especially first 2 weeks), nausea, upper respiratory tract infection, headache/tension headache, vomiting. Serious: severe diarrhea/nausea/vomiting (most in first weeks of treatment). Depression and suicidal behavior (rare but labeling includes warning). Weight loss. Post-marketing: angioedema, anaphylaxis. |
| **Patient support programs** | Amgen SupportPlus (1-844-4OTEZLA / 1-844-468-3952). Otezla Co-Pay Program ($0/month for eligible commercially insured). Amgen Nurse Partners. Amgen Safety Net Foundation (free drug for qualifying uninsured). |
| **Competitors** | **Skyrizi (risankizumab)** -- AbbVie. Anti-IL-23. Biologic. Superior efficacy in head-to-head vs Otezla. **Sotyktu (deucravacitinib)** -- BMS. TYK2 inhibitor. Oral. Outperformed Otezla in head-to-head trial for moderate-to-severe psoriasis. Key competitive threat. **Cosentyx (secukinumab)** -- Novartis. Anti-IL-17A. Biologic. **Humira (adalimumab) / biosimilars** -- AbbVie. Anti-TNF. Now off-patent. Otezla's differentiator: only oral option across all psoriasis severities, no immunosuppression, no lab monitoring required. |

## Product 5: BLINCYTO (blinatumomab)

| Field | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Brand / Generic** | BLINCYTO / blinatumomab |
| **Therapeutic area** | Oncology -- Hematology |
| **FY2025 revenue** | ~$1,559M (+28% YoY) |
| **Mechanism** | Bispecific T-cell engager (BiTE) antibody construct. Binds CD19 on B-cells and CD3 on T-cells simultaneously, redirecting T-cells to attack CD19-positive leukemia cells. |
| **Indications** | (1) CD19-positive Philadelphia chromosome-negative B-cell precursor ALL in the consolidation phase of multiphase chemotherapy (adults and pediatric patients 1 month+). (2) CD19-positive B-cell precursor ALL with MRD >=0.1% in first or second complete remission (adults and pediatric patients 1 month+). (3) Relapsed or refractory CD19-positive B-cell precursor ALL (adults and pediatric patients 1 month+). |
| **Patient population** | Adults and children (1 month+) with B-cell precursor acute lymphoblastic leukemia. Consolidation, MRD-positive, or relapsed/refractory settings. Often younger patients -- ALL is the most common pediatric cancer. Adult ALL patients tend to be younger adults or elderly with poor prognosis. |
| **Route** | Continuous intravenous (IV) infusion via programmable, lockable infusion pump. First cycle: 28-day continuous infusion. Requires hospitalization for first 9 days of first cycle and first 2 days of second cycle. |
| **Prescribing HCP specialties** | Hematologist-oncologists, pediatric oncologists |
| **Key screening/monitoring** | CD19 status confirmed by flow cytometry or immunohistochemistry. Minimal residual disease (MRD) testing (flow cytometry or PCR, sensitivity >=10^-4). Philadelphia chromosome status (FISH or cytogenetics). CBC with differential (monitor for cytopenia). Liver function tests. CRP and cytokine panels (CRS monitoring). Neurological assessments (ICANS monitoring). |
| **Common adverse events** | Pyrexia, infusion-related reactions, headache, infection, musculoskeletal pain, neutropenia, nausea, anemia, thrombocytopenia, diarrhea. **BOXED WARNING:** Cytokine release syndrome (CRS) -- 7-15% of patients, can be life-threatening. Neurological toxicities/ICANS -- ~65% of patients in trials. Serious infections (sepsis, pneumonia, bacteremia) -- ~25%. |
| **Patient support programs** | Amgen Assist 360 (oncology hub). Amgen FIRST STEP (copay/deductible coverage, $0 first dose). Amgen Nurse Ambassadors (enrollment via authorization form). Amgen Safety Net Foundation. Phone: 1-866-264-2778. |
| **Competitors** | **Besponsa (inotuzumab ozogamicin)** -- Pfizer. Anti-CD22 antibody-drug conjugate. Approved for R/R B-ALL in adults. Advantage: effective regardless of disease burden. **Kymriah (tisagenlecleucel)** -- Novartis. CAR-T cell therapy. Approved for pediatric/young adult R/R B-ALL. One-time infusion but requires manufacturing time. **Tecartus (brexucabtagene autoleucel)** -- Kite/Gilead. CAR-T for adult R/R B-ALL. BLINCYTO's differentiator: off-the-shelf (no manufacturing wait), used across consolidation/MRD/R-R settings, and increasingly used as bridge to transplant or CAR-T. |

## Product 6: EVENITY (romosozumab-aqqg)

| Field | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Brand / Generic** | EVENITY / romosozumab-aqqg |
| **Therapeutic area** | Bone health / Osteoporosis |
| **FY2025 revenue** | ~$2,100M (+34% YoY) |
| **Mechanism** | Humanized monoclonal antibody; sclerostin inhibitor. Blocks sclerostin, which normally inhibits bone formation. Dual effect: increases bone formation AND decreases bone resorption. Only anabolic agent with this dual mechanism. |
| **Indications** | Treatment of osteoporosis in postmenopausal women at high risk for fracture, defined as: history of osteoporotic fracture, multiple risk factors for fracture, or patients who have failed or are intolerant to other available osteoporosis therapy. |
| **Patient population** | Postmenopausal women with severe osteoporosis and high fracture risk. NOT for patients who have had MI or stroke in the preceding year (CV risk). Typically 55-85 years old. Treatment limited to 12 monthly doses (anabolic effect wanes after 12 months), then transition to antiresorptive therapy (e.g., denosumab or bisphosphonate). |
| **Route** | Subcutaneous injection, 210 mg once monthly (two 105 mg injections per visit) for 12 months. Administered in HCP office. |
| **Prescribing HCP specialties** | Rheumatologists, endocrinologists, orthopedic surgeons, primary care physicians, OB/GYNs |
| **Key screening/monitoring** | DEXA scan (bone mineral density at baseline and after 12-month course). Serum calcium (correct hypocalcemia before starting). Cardiovascular risk assessment (CONTRAINDICATED if MI or stroke within prior year -- BOXED WARNING). Vitamin D levels. Dental assessment (ONJ risk, though lower than with denosumab). |
| **Common adverse events** | Arthralgia, headache, injection site reactions. **BOXED WARNING: Major adverse cardiac events (MACE)** -- higher rate of CV death, nonfatal MI, and nonfatal stroke vs alendronate in clinical trial. Should not be initiated in patients with MI or stroke in prior year. Hypocalcemia. ONJ (rare). Atypical femoral fractures (rare). |
| **Patient support programs** | Amgen SupportPlus Co-Pay Program ($0 copay eligible). Bone Matters support program. Amgen Nurse Partners. Amgen Safety Net Foundation. |
| **Competitors** | **Forteo (teriparatide)** -- Eli Lilly. PTH analog. Daily SC injection for 18-24 months. Now has biosimilars available. **Tymlos (abaloparatide)** -- Radius Health. PTH-related peptide analog. Daily SC injection for 18-24 months. EVENITY's differentiator: dual mechanism (anabolic + anti-resorptive), monthly dosing (vs daily for competitors), 12-month course (vs 18-24 months). |

## Additional Major Products (Brief Profiles)

### Enbrel (etanercept) -- Autoimmune/Inflammation

| Field | Detail |
|---|---|
| **FY2025 revenue** | ~$2,226M (-33% YoY, steep decline) |
| **Status** | Still marketed by Amgen in US/Canada (Pfizer markets outside US/Canada). Facing steep price erosion from Medicare Part D redesign, 340B program expansion. CMS negotiated price 67% below list (effective Jan 2026). Patents likely extend monopoly to 2029. |
| **Indications** | Rheumatoid arthritis, psoriatic arthritis, ankylosing spondylitis, plaque psoriasis, polyarticular juvenile idiopathic arthritis |
| **Route** | SC injection, 50 mg weekly (or 25 mg twice weekly) |
| **Competitors** | Humira (adalimumab) + biosimilars, Remicade (infliximab) + biosimilars, Rinvoq (upadacitinib), Xeljanz (tofacitinib) |
| **Note for demo** | Declining asset. Revenue falling fast due to government price negotiation. May not be ideal as a flagship demo product. |

### LUMAKRAS (sotorasib) -- Oncology

| Field | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Therapeutic area** | Oncology -- NSCLC and mCRC |
| **Mechanism** | Small molecule; KRAS G12C inhibitor. First-in-class. Covalently binds KRAS G12C, locks it in inactive GDP-bound state. |
| **Indications** | (1) KRAS G12C-mutated locally advanced or metastatic NSCLC (accelerated approval, after >=1 prior systemic therapy). (2) In combination with Vectibix (panitumumab) for chemorefractory KRAS G12C-mutated metastatic colorectal cancer (FDA approved Jan 2025). |
| **Route** | Oral tablet, 960 mg (eight 120 mg tablets) once daily. |
| **Key screening** | **KRAS G12C mutation testing** (FDA-approved companion diagnostic required). Liver function tests at baseline and periodically (hepatotoxicity in 27%, Grade >=3 in 16%). Pulmonary assessment (ILD/pneumonitis in 2.2%). |
| **Common AEs** | Diarrhea, musculoskeletal pain, nausea, fatigue, hepatotoxicity, cough. Serious: hepatotoxicity (median onset 6.3 weeks), ILD/pneumonitis (1 fatal case reported). |
| **Patient support** | Amgen Assist 360. Amgen FIRST STEP. Amgen Nurse Ambassadors. |
| **Competitors** | **Krazati (adagrasib)** -- Mirati/BMS. KRAS G12C inhibitor. Accelerated approval for NSCLC and CRC. Different AE profile. **JDQ443** -- Novartis. In development. |

### IMDELLTRA (tarlatamab-dlle) -- Oncology (Rising Star)

| Field | Detail |
|---|---|
| **FY2025 revenue** | $627M (rapid launch trajectory) |
| **Mechanism** | Bispecific T-cell engager (BiTE); targets DLL3 on tumor cells and CD3 on T-cells |
| **Indication** | Extensive-stage small cell lung cancer (ES-SCLC) after progression on platinum-based chemotherapy |
| **Route** | IV infusion |
| **Note** | Fastest-growing new oncology launch for Amgen. DLL3 is expressed in ~80% of SCLC. |
